friedrice

Description of the dish. A few lines describing the recipe's background or inspiration.

Ingredients:

  • oil
  • onions
  • Long Grain Rice
  • Ginger
  • Tomatoes
  • Carrots
  • Salt
  • Bay Leaves
  • Curry Powder
  • Seasoning Cubes
  • Tomato Paste
  • Scotch Bonnet Pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: • Use day-old rice for the best fried rice. If you don’t have leftover rice, cook the rice and let it cool completely. This helps the rice grains to separate and not become mushy when fried.
  2. Prepare the Ingredients: • Chop the onions, bell peppers, and carrots. • Mince the garlic and ginger (if using). • Beat the eggs in a bowl and set them aside. • If using any cooked meat (chicken, shrimp, beef, or tofu), cut it into small cubes or strips. • Chop the spring onions (for garnish) and prepare any other optional ingredients like peas, sweet corn, or scotch bonnet pepper.
  3. Cook the Eggs: • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium heat. • Pour in the beaten eggs and cook them, stirring gently until scrambled and cooked through. Remove the eggs from the pan and set aside.
  4. Sauté the Vegetables: • In the same pan, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil. • Add the chopped onions, garlic, and ginger (if using), and sauté for about 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant. • Add the carrots, bell peppers, peas (if using), and any other vegetables you like. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still slightly crisp.
  5. Add the Rice: • Add the cold, cooked rice to the pan with the sautéed vegetables. Use a spatula or spoon to break up any clumps of rice and stir everything together. • Stir-fry the rice for about 5-7 minutes, making sure the rice is well-coated with oil and mixed with the vegetables.
  6. Season the Rice: • Pour in the soy sauce and add any seasoning cubes (if using), salt, and black pepper to taste. • Stir everything well to evenly distribute the seasonings throughout the rice. • If you like, add a small drizzle of sesame oil for extra flavor.
  7. Add Protein (Optional): • If using cooked meat (like chicken, beef, or shrimp), add it to the pan and stir it into the rice, allowing it to heat through.
  8. Add the Cooked Eggs: • Gently fold the scrambled eggs back into the rice, mixing them evenly with the rest of the ingredients.
  9. Final Stir-Fry: • Stir-fry everything together for another 3-5 minutes, ensuring the rice is hot and all the ingredients are well combined. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  10. Garnish and Serve: • Garnish the fried rice with freshly chopped spring onions and any optional toppings like cilantro or more chili if you prefer extra spice. • Serve hot and enjoy your delicious fried rice!

Jellof Rice

Description of the dish. A few lines describing the recipe's background or inspiration.

Ingredients:

  • Long Grain Parboiled Rice
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Red Bell Pepper
  • Ginger
  • Oil
  • Seasoning Cubes
  • Salt
  • Curry powder
  • Bay leaves
  • Thyme

Instructions:

  1. Gather your ingredients, including long-grain rice, tomatoes (fresh or canned), onions, bell peppers, garlic, ginger, scotch bonnet (optional for spice), vegetable oil, chicken or beef broth (optional), and seasonings like curry powder, thyme, and bay leaves.
  2. Blend the Tomatoes and Peppers: • Blend the tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, garlic, ginger, and scotch bonnet (if using) into a smooth puree.
  3. Fry the Tomato Paste: • Heat oil in a large pot and fry the tomato paste until it darkens slightly, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  4. Add the Tomato Puree: • Add the blended tomato mixture to the fried tomato paste. Let it simmer for 15-20 minutes until it thickens and the raw taste of the tomatoes is cooked out.
  5. Season the Sauce: • Add seasonings such as curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt, and any other desired spices. Stir well and adjust seasoning to taste.
  6. Add Broth or Water: • Add chicken or beef broth (or water) to the sauce and bring it to a boil. This will provide moisture for the rice to cook in.
  7. Add Broth or Water: • Add chicken or beef broth (or water) to the sauce and bring it to a boil. This will provide moisture for the rice to cook in.
  8. Simmer the Rice: • Cover the pot, reduce the heat to low, and let the rice cook. Stir occasionally to prevent burning and add more broth or water if necessary to ensure the rice cooks evenly and absorbs the flavors
  9. Check the Rice: • After about 20-30 minutes, check the rice for doneness. If the rice is not fully cooked and there’s still liquid, cover it again and allow it to cook longer.
